Ganesh Chaturthi is a major Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity symbolizing wisdom, prosperity, and remover of obstacles. It typically lasts 10 days, beginning on the fourth day of the Hindu month of Bhadrapada (August/September). The festival starts with the installation of Ganesha idols in homes and public pandals (temporary structures). Devotees perform daily prayers, offer sweets like modak (Ganesha's favorite), and sing hymns in his honor. Communities come together for cultural events, music, and dance performances during the festival.

Holi, also known as the "Festival of Colors," is a vibrant Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. It typically takes place in March on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Phalguna. The festival begins with Holika Dahan, a bonfire symbolizing the burning of the demoness Holika and the triumph of good over evil. The next day, people of all ages come together to throw colored powders (gulal) and water at each other in joyous celebration. It is a time for social harmony, as friends, family, and even strangers come together, breaking barriers of caste, class, and age. Music, dancing, and festive foods like gujiya and thandai are an integral part of the celebration.
